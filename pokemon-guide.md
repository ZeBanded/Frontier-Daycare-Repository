# Pokemon Guide

This guide outlines the most efficient ways to obtain each target Pokemon, listed in Pokedex order. Games for each target are sorted from most to least efficient.

> [!NOTE]
> This guide contains information for FireRed and LeafGreen. At the time of writing, it is *not yet possible* to transfer Pokemon from these games to HOME. The information is still listed for posterity.

## Initial notes and tips

- Game initials:
    - LGPE = Let's Go! Pikachu/Eevee
    - SwSh = Sword/Shield
    - BDSP = Brilliant Diamond / Shining Pearl
    - PLA = Legends: Arceus
    - SV = Scarlet/Violet
    - Z-A = Legends: Z-A
    - FRLG = FireRed/LeafGreen
    - "DLC" or "+ DLC" means that game's DLC is required
- Encounter rates less than or equal to 5% are not listed unless they're the only repeatable method for that game.
- Breeding is almost always very easy and, in modern games, allows forcing a specific Nature and Ability. For many Pokemon, this will be very efficient and result in some VP savings; however, for some, it will be slow due to long egg cycles. These are the Switch games with breeding, from most to least efficient:
    - SV
    - SwSh
    - BDSP
    - FRLG
- LGPE allows [forcing a specific Nature](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Celadon_City#Fortune_Teller) for *all spawns* for the day. Paired with [Catch Combos](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Catch_Combo), this is a very efficient way to catch Kanto Pokemon with the correct Nature to save some VP.
- Champions does not use IVs, so you don't need to worry about them.
- Moves often don't take much effort to change, and all signs point to a Pokemon's moves remaining intact in Champions so long as they're implemented, so it's recommended to set at least some desired moves to help players save VP.
- SV spawner maps for Paldea and Kitakami can be found [here](https://spo.ink/sv_maps) ([source](https://x.com/vbbcla/status/1706057570652242371)).

## Venusaur

Available in LGPE, SwSh + DLC, BDSP, SV + DLC, Z-A, FRLG, and GO.

This line is generally best bred.

- **LGPE:** Bulbasaur can be found as a rare spawn in [Viridian Forest](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Viridian_Forest).
- **SV + DLC:** Bulbasaur can be found in the [Torchlit Mountain](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Coastal_Biome#Torchlit_Mountain) area of the Coastal Biome. Making a Poison Encounter Sandwich will cause it to always spawn from [Rocky Area spawners](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Biome#Rocky_Area). It can also appear as a [mass outbreak](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/List_of_mass_outbreaks_in_the_Terarium).
- **SwSh + DLC:** Venusaur can be found in Max Raid Battles at the Forest of Focus, in [Den A](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Forest_of_Focus/Dens#A). One Bulbasaur is also a gift option in the Isle of Armor storyline.
- **FRLG:** Bulbasaur is a starter Pokemon choice.

## Charizard

Available in all Switch games and GO.

This line is generally best bred.

- **LGPE:** Charizard can be found flying in the sky in all Routes except 5, 20, and 26+. Charmander can be found as a rare spawn in [Routes 3](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kanto_Route_3) and [4](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kanto_Route_4), and [Rock Tunnel](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Rock_Tunnel).
- **SV + DLC:** WIP
- **Z-A + DLC:** The entire evolutionary line can be found in [Fire-type hyperspace wild zones](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/List_of_Fire-type_hyperspace_wild_zones).
- **Z-A:** Charmander can be found in [Wild Zone 20](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Wild_Zone_20).
- **SwSh:** One Charmander is available as a gift after the main story is completed. Charizard is available in Max Raid Battles:
    - At Hammerlocke Hills, it has a 5% chance to appear in purple beams at [Den D](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Hammerlocke_Hills/Dens#D).
    - At the Lake of Outrage, it has a 5% chance to appear in purple beams at [Den C](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Lake_of_Outrage/Dens#C).
- **FRLG:** Charmander is a starter Pokemon choice.

## Ursaluna

Only available in PLA in Massive Mass Outbreaks, or by evolving from Ursaring. The latter is significantly more efficient, as [Peat Blocks can easily be farmed](/PLA/ursaluna-dig-farm.md).

To evolve Ursaring, sleep until night until it becomes a full moon, then use the Peat Block.

### Ursaring

- **PLA:**
    - Two Alpha Ursaring [always appear](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Alpha_Pok%C3%A9mon#Crimson_Mirelands) in Crimson Mirelands. Teddiursa can often be found near them, too.
    - Ursaring and Teddiursa can be found along the [Sonorous Path](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Sonorous_Path).
- **SV:** WIP
- **Shining Pearl:** Teddiursa appears in the Grand Underground.

## Sneasler

Outside of PLA Massive Mass Outbreaks, Sneasler is obtained by evolving Hisuian Sneasel, which can only be initially obtained in PLA.

This line is generally best bred.

- **SV + PLA:** Transfer Hisuian Sneasel into SV from PLA, breed it while it's holding an Everstone, and purchase Razor Claws from Delibird Presents in Mesagoza to evolve them.
- **PLA:** Hisuian Sneasel can be found in Coronet Highlands and Alabaster Icelands. Razor Claws can be bought for 1400 MP.

## Meowscarada

Available in SV and GO by evolving Sprigatito.

This line is generally best bred.

- **HOME:** You get all three unevolved Paldea Starters when you deposit a Pokemon from SV for the first time.
- **SV:** Sprigatito is a starter Pokemon choice.

## Farigiraf

Only available in SV.

Girafarig can be evolved into Farigiraf by teaching it Twin Beam, then leveling it up.

- **SV:** Both Girafarig and Farigiraf can be found in [Area Zero](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Area_Zero).

## Gholdengo

Available in SV, Z-A + DLC, and GO, only by evolving Gimmighoul.

To evolve Gimmighoul, [have 999 Coins](/SV/gimmighoul-coins.md), then induce the evolution, either by leveling it up in SV or using the menu in Z-A and GO.

### Gimmighoul

- **SV:** Chest Form Gimmighoul can be found in certain spots throughout Paldea, most easily atop watchtowers. One Chest Form Gimmighoul has a 5% chance of respawning every in-game day.
- **Z-A + DLC:** Chest Form Gimmighoul can be found as a Special Scan in Hyperspace Lumiose.
- **GO + SV:** Roaming Form Gimmighoul can be found by using the Coin Bag once per day, which is obtained and refreshed daily by linking with SV. This cannot be dateskipped, as the daily check is performed in GO at midnight local time.

## Archaludon

Only available in SV + DLC by evolving Duraludon.

To evolve Duraludon, use a Metal Alloy on it. Metal Alloys can easily be farmed using [Item Printer RNG manipulation](/SV/printer-manip.md).

### Duraludon

Duraludon is available in SwSh, as well as SV + DLC.

- **SV + DLC:** Encounter Power Steel sandwich in Polar Biome. WIP
- **SwSh:**
    - Guaranteed daily wanderer at [Giant's Seat](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Giant%27s_Seat#Wanderers)
    - Gigantamax sometimes appears in any red-beam Max Raid Battle from [Wild Area News](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Wild_Area_News#November_1,_2022,_to_End-of-life)
    - **DLC:** Purple-beam Max Raid Battles at Path to the Peak [Den B](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Path_to_the_Peak/Dens#B)
